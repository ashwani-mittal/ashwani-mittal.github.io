Top 5 Reasons to Join AIESEC

January 7, 2015 by Allison

Top 5 Reasons to Join AIESEC

  1. We have a super easy name and acronym…right?

Even though our name can get a little confusing, it shows just how global of an organization we are. For those of you who don’t know, AIESEC was created after World War II when students like us realized that our world could not take something like that again. So they made this organization to make a cross-cultural experience so students have the opportunity to grow into more open people. We are founded in 124 countries and territories and strive to make unique internships all over the world for students to participate in. We are student run and we have already accomplished so much. Our acronym might be in French, but we are global. For those that are curious, here is the acronym…

A-Association

I-internationale des

E– étudiants en

S– sciences

E– économiques et

C– commerciales

  1. You gain skills that class can’t teach you.

AIESEC basically works like a business. Your local committee (Your college campus) works as one of the branches. Because of this, you are going to learn so many new things such as marketing techniques, business etiquette, account delivery, finance, and management. Speaking from experience, I have learned so much about marketing already even though I have yet to take one business class in my college career. AIESEC simply puts you a couple steps ahead of everyone else: not only are you learning new skills but you are applying them in real world experience and leadership experiences.

  1. You meet so many new people from all over the world.

Being in AIESEC exposes you to so many new people from all other the country and the world- from all different cultures and backgrounds. Whether it’s in your Local Committee, a National Conference, or an International Conference, you are sure to meet people from all corners of the world. You get to meet people who speak multiple languages, have the most interesting international stories, and have family traditions completely different from your own. Not only do you gain new friends, but you gain a more open ideal of different people.

  1. You not only develop as a leader, but you gain professional experience.

AIESEC gives you the chance to gain leadership and professional experience that is quite different from any other organization that you might have an opportunity to join during your college career. AIESEC gives you the opportunity to talk to businesses and business professionals first hand. We as students are running this business. We are marketing towards other students, finding sponsors for our annual Youth to Business Forum, sending students abroad on internships, bringing other students to the United States for internships. We send people to different countries on internships that will change their lives. We do so many remarkable and unique things that can really change your job-applying game.

  1. You are working towards making a large and global impact.

If you are becoming a better leader and thousands of other college students are doing the same thing, are we not working towards a greater movement? If we are sending people abroad, seeing the world, meeting people from the other side of the world, how can we not make a difference? One leader at a time, we are making this world into a more peaceful and less ignorant one. One of the greatest things about this organization is that we believe in the why. We know that if we create these leaders and these experiences abroad, we can help change the world and the way we have always thought about things. And that, in itself is something to be seen.

So what are you waiting for? How will you impact the world?

Allison is a first-year International Business and Marketing Co-major at UGA. She also happens to be a chronic over-packer and over-sharer. She is an avid gymnastics watcher and notorious dancer. You can find her in dorm room watching Harry Potter while eating Jolly Ranchers.
